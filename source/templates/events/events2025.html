Project Diliverance 2025
How we returned

Returning to Timor

In 2025, God called our group to return to Timor for another mission trip. However, with the boys in NS and the girls being caught up in their schoolwork, many of us were willing but unable to commit to this trip.

Despite this, we continued to pray and eventually found enough members who could join us on the trip. The rest of us who were unable to go continued to support the team as senders.

Going Into The Mission Field

Joining Cocoon's Youth Club

This year, we returned to Cocoon's Youth Club to run a youth programme. We performed a skit about loving who are outcast or who may be different from us.

We invited the youths to share their thoughts on the skit and how they related to it. Our hearts were broken when we heard about the ostracism they faced, but we were encouraged by the fact that they felt loved and welcomed in Cocoon.

The Cocoon Youth Club staff also brought us out on a hike! We climbed up a hill and drove down to a beach to swim, taking time to enjoy and appreciate the natural wonders that God created.

Going Into The Missions Field

Cocoon's Preschool Sessions

We also had the chance to join one of the preschool sessions, where we shared the creation story with them and got them to colour in some activity sheets. We also sang songs with them!

I turned to a small Timorese boy with albinism and told him, "You are good!" The boy then started shaking his head vigorously and muttering "no" in response. The other kids around me also turned to me and said, "He is malai (foreigner in Tetum)," and my heart simply broke. Seeing a small boy at a young age (of around 2-4 years old?) being outcasted and already having the belief that he is not good reminded me of how important it is for our generation to step up and nurture the next generation, instilling good values and just showing these children God's love while they are young. - Keiko
